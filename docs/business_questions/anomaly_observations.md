# Stock Market Anomaly Observations

## Objective

The purpose of this analysis was to identify abnormal stock price movements and volatility spikes across NIFTY 50 companies using historical trading data from 2000–2023.

The anomaly detection process focused on identifying trading periods where stock movements deviated significantly from rolling historical behavior.

---

# Key Observations

- Multiple companies demonstrated sudden volatility spikes during periods of broader market instability.
- Certain stocks experienced recurring abnormal return patterns over multiple time windows.
- Market anomalies were more concentrated during high-uncertainty economic periods.
- Extreme price movements were often accompanied by elevated trading activity and volatility expansion.

---

# Rolling Volatility Anomalies

The rolling 30-day volatility analysis identified periods where short-term market instability increased sharply relative to historical averages.

These periods indicate:
- elevated investor uncertainty,
- rapid price fluctuations,
- and increased market sensitivity.

---

# Price Movement Outliers

Daily return analysis identified abnormal trading sessions where stock returns deviated significantly from expected movement ranges.

Potential drivers of these anomalies may include:
- earnings announcements,
- macroeconomic events,
- sector-specific developments,
- or sudden shifts in investor sentiment.

---

# Comparative Market Behavior

Not all companies reacted equally during volatile periods.

Certain companies consistently demonstrated:
- higher volatility persistence,
- stronger abnormal movement frequency,
- and larger return deviations.

This suggests varying levels of market stability and risk exposure across companies.

---

# Business Interpretation

Anomaly detection provides valuable insight into:
- market instability,
- portfolio risk exposure,
- and unusual trading behavior.

From a business intelligence perspective, identifying abnormal market behavior can support:
- risk monitoring,
- investment analysis,
- volatility tracking,
- and market event investigation.

---

# Recommendations

- Monitor recurring high-volatility stocks more closely during uncertain market conditions.
- Combine anomaly detection with sector-level analysis for deeper insights.
- Extend the model with trading volume anomaly detection.
- Incorporate predictive analytics for forward-looking volatility forecasting.
